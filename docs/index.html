5 · What to do with this

    The science in four lines

    ε = ṡ·L / (Δh₀·ū)  ·  ε* = 2(λ₁(Pe)+Da)/[ζ(1+0.12α₀)]  ·  λ₁ = π²/Pe + Pe/4  ·  ε_eff = R·ε

    ε measures how fast sea-level rise strengthens the saltwater wedge relative to the aquifer's natural flushing speed; ε* is the analytically derived rate above which wedge-driven trapping outruns flushing. Because the wedge also boosts mean seaward throughflow (μ₁ = ζPe/4 > 0), aquifer-average cleanup actually accelerates under sea-level rise while the shoreline zone deteriorates — which is exactly why site-average monitoring misses the problem, and why ε* is a conservative (protective) threshold.

    Trust window

    Benchmarked against full nonlinear variable-density simulations: local predictions within 5% up to coupling α ≈ 1.0 and 15% up to α ≈ 1.8; the flags in step 2 tell you when your inputs leave that envelope. In flux-controlled systems the theory correctly predicts insensitivity (ε ≈ 0), per Chang et al. (2011) and Sadhasivam et al. (2025).
